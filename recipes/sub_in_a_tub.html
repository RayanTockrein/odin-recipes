Sub in a Tub

Credit: Sara Haas

Description

Most online versions of the salad begin with a big bowl of chopped romaine lettuce, t
opped with various deli meats and cheeses, tomatoes, onion, oregano, and salt, before it’s
dressed with red wine vinegar and olive oil.
Then it’s time for customization! Use this as a base and add whatever other vegetables or
protein you like.

Jersey Mike’s Copycat “Sub in a Tub”:

Inspired, I set out to create my version, embracing the classic sub sandwich flavors,
but with a few twists to make it more me. I kept the foundation of lettuce, tomato, onion, deli meats,
and cheeses. I also kept the seasoning and simple olive oil and red wine vinegar dressing.
I tossed in some sliced banana peppers for a little bit of heat and acidity.
I’m also a big crouton fan, so I made my own from French bread (store-bought works, too).
Finally, I added some green bell pepper, because it just felt right, and olives because I love the
pop of briny saltiness.

Ingredients:

For the salad:

  • 8 cups chopped romaine
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced or diced
  • 1/2 cup cured meat or deli meat, sliced or diced
  • 1 cup diced cheese
  • 1/2 cup jarred pepperoncinis, sliced
  • 1/2 cup pitted green olives
  • 2 cups croutons

For the dressing:

  • 1/4 cup red
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the lettuce to a large serving bowl along with the remaining salad ingredients.
  2. Whisk the vinegar, oil, oregano, salt, and black pepper together in a small
    bowl or add to a lidded jar and shake to combine. Pour over salad and toss
    until ingredients are well-coated. Enjoy immediately.
